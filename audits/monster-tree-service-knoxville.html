Monster Tree Service of Knoxville Google Business Profile Review

Reviews are one of Monster Tree Service of Knoxville's biggest strengths. They have 523 reviews, which is a very strong total, and more importantly, they appear to be getting consistent new reviews. For service-based businesses, we always recommend aiming for at least two to three new reviews per week. It is not the total count that matters most — it is the velocity. A business with 200 reviews but three new ones this week will outrank a business with 800 reviews that stopped coming in six months ago. Monster Tree Service of Knoxville is doing a great job here.

Photos are also in good shape. They have a solid number of photos, which increases engagement from potential customers browsing the profile and sends activity signals to Google. We recommend different types of photos — completed jobs, before-and-after shots, crew at work, equipment — and geotagging each one with GPS coordinates from the job site. While geotagging is debated in the SEO industry, we always recommend putting in the extra effort. It helps with proximity-based searches and there is no downside. This is an area where Monster Tree Service of Knoxville is already ahead of most competitors, similar to what we found in the Voluntrees audit where photo activity was also strong.

Their GBP category is set to one — which is exactly right. We recommend having at most three categories, and ideally just one primary category that matches your main service. Monster Tree Service of Knoxville is not diluting their ranking signal here, and that is a genuine strength. Some businesses load up categories thinking it helps, but in most cases it hurts. The rare exceptions are businesses that have optimized everything else so well that the extra categories do not cause confusion.

When it comes to services, Google allows up to 99. We recommend filling in at least 30 with descriptions for the top 20. This is how you capture the broad spectrum of search queries. Someone searching "stump grinding Knoxville" or "emergency tree removal" will only find Monster Tree Service of Knoxville through the services section, not through the category alone. If this is not fully built out, it is an easy win.

The booking link is set up and leads to their contact page, which is good. They also have a lead capture automated chat system on their website — that is a strong move that reduces friction and gives potential customers another way to reach out immediately. Beyond categories, services, and booking, we always recommend completing every single question and attribute Google asks. Each answer gives the algorithm more content to work with and increases the profile's trustworthiness.

Monster Tree Service of Knoxville NAP Consistency

NAP — Name, Address, and Phone Number — needs to be identical across every online presence. On the Monster Tree Service of Knoxville website, the address shows "Hayfield Road" with "Road" spelled out completely. If their Google Business Profile or any other listing abbreviates it as "Rd," that inconsistency confuses Google. It seems like a minor detail, but Google treats NAP consistency as a direct trust signal. The fix is straightforward: check the GBP spelling, then match it exactly on the website, Facebook, Instagram, Yelp, and every other platform. We found the same type of issue in the Scott's Plumbing Company audit where "Drive" versus "DR" and "#5" versus "STE 5" were creating unnecessary doubt in Google's algorithm.
