How Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding Ranks on Google Right Now

We started by Googling "Anthony's Hughes Tree and Stump Grinding" to see what comes up. Their website and business information do rank first for their own business name, which is the baseline expectation.

For the main keyword search — "tree service Knoxville Tennessee" — Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding does not appear in the first batch of results at all. They are not on the first page of the Google Maps pack, which means they are essentially invisible to potential customers who are actively searching for tree services in Knoxville. The top 3 businesses in the map pack — Hancock Tree Service, Tip Top Tree, and Smoky Mountain Tree Service — are capturing more than 50 to 60% of all clicks from those searchers. Every day that Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding is not showing up in those results is a day they are losing potential customers to competitors.

The good news is that this also means there is significant room for growth. The gaps we found are fixable, and most of them are straightforward.

Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding Google Business Profile Review

Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding has 58 reviews. That number on its own is not the issue — what matters more is the velocity. Their most recent review was posted over a year ago. From a ranking perspective, this is a problem. Google weights recent, consistent reviews far more heavily than total count. A competitor with 30 reviews that are all from the past three months will outrank a business with 200 reviews that stopped coming in a year ago.

For tree service businesses in a market like Knoxville, we recommend building a review generation system. For contrast, L&M Tree Service has 299 reviews with healthy velocity and ranks 12th — that is the kind of review engine Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding needs to build. We recommend building a review generation system that produces at least 2–3 new Google reviews per week. This does not happen by accident — it requires a process. Something as simple as sending a follow-up text with a review link 24 hours after completing a job can make a massive difference over time. Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding needs to restart that review flow as a top priority.

Their photos are all real, which is good — no stock images. However, many of the photos appear to be similar or repeating. Google and customers both benefit from diversity in photo types: completed projects, before-and-after shots, crew members at work, equipment, vehicles, and the business exterior. Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding should add more varied photos and geotag each one with GPS coordinates from the job sites where the work was done. Geotagging tells Google exactly where the business operates and strengthens relevance for searches in those specific neighborhoods.

Their GBP category is set to just Tree Service, which is the right approach. We recommend one primary category at most three. Having too many categories dilutes Google's understanding of what the business actually does. Adding Arborist Service as a secondary category could be beneficial, but one category is perfectly fine.

The biggest gap on the GBP is the services section. Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding does not appear to have any services listed. Google allows up to 99 services, and we recommend loading at least 30 with descriptions for the top 20. This is one of the most commonly missed opportunities we see in our audits. Every service added is a new keyword that Google can match to customer searches — things like "stump grinding Knoxville" or "emergency storm tree removal near me." Without services listed, Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding is invisible for all of those specific queries.

Beyond services, the overall GBP profile completeness needs significant attention. The business is missing hours, a full description, attributes, a booking link, and Q&A / FAQ entries. Google treats profile completeness as a trust signal — the more information they have, the more confident they are in showing the business to searchers. Every empty field is a missed opportunity. Adding a booking link alone would capture more leads directly from Google without visitors even needing to click through to the website.

Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding Website SEO Review

The Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding website title tag is fine — it includes the location, service, and business name. We would not change it.

The H1 tag is where we found an issue. The H1 is currently set to just the business name, and the text that includes the keyword plus location is tagged as an H2 instead. For local SEO, the H1 should be the most prominent heading on the page and it should include the primary keyword and city — something like "Tree Service Knoxville TN — Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding." Swapping the H1 and H2 assignments would be a simple but impactful fix.

A major gap is the missing NAP on the website. Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding does not appear to have their business address displayed anywhere on their website. On their GBP, the address uses the abbreviation "WY" — and that exact formatting needs to appear on the website as well. We would recommend adding the full name, address, and phone number to the website footer so it appears on every page, using the identical spelling and abbreviations as the GBP. Alongside the address, embedding a Google Map showing the business location would send Google another confirmation signal about where the business operates.

NAP consistency is a foundational ranking factor. If the address does not match exactly across the website, GBP, Facebook, Yelp, and every other directory, Google cannot be certain which version is correct. That uncertainty directly costs rankings.

Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding has only 6 indexed pages on their website. We recommend a minimum of 30 indexed pages, ideally 50 or more. Voluntrees, another tree service we audited, has only 1 indexed page and faces the same content challenge. Those pages should include dedicated service pages for each type of work the business performs — tree removal, stump grinding, land clearing, storm damage cleanup — as well as area pages targeting specific neighborhoods and towns in the Knoxville metro. Right now, if someone searches "how to remove trees from my backyard in Knoxville," Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding has nothing to show for it. Every missing page is a missed opportunity to capture a customer who is already looking for the exact service they provide.

Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding Technical SEO

We ran the website through Google PageSpeed Insights. The mobile score is not terrible but has room for improvement. Page speed is a confirmed Google ranking factor, and it also affects bounce rate — visitors leave slow sites. Optimizing the backend code through image compression, lazy loading, and CSS/JS minification would improve both the score and the user experience.

The site does have a valid SSL certificate (HTTPS), which is a non-negotiable trust and ranking requirement.

Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding Citations & Local Listings

Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding has citations on Yelp, Facebook, Yahoo, and a few smaller directories. The foundation exists but the citation profile is thin compared to the top-ranking competitors. Building consistent listings on Apple Business Connect, Bing Places, Nextdoor, MapQuest, Foursquare, Yellow Pages, BBB, and tree service-specific directories would strengthen the trust signal that Google uses to validate the business. Each new consistent citation — where the name, address, and phone number match the GBP exactly — is another vote of confidence in the listing's legitimacy.

What Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding Is Doing Well

Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding does have some positives to build on. They rank first for their own business name. Their photos are real, not stock. Their GBP category is correctly set to just Tree Service without over-categorization. Their title tag is properly structured. And they have a valid SSL certificate. These are the basics done right — the challenge is that many of the intermediate steps have not been taken yet, which is why they are not showing up in competitive search results.

Top Recommendations for Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding

1. Complete the Google Business Profile. This is the single highest priority. Fill in every field — hours, description, attributes, booking link, Q&A. Load at least 30 services with descriptions for the top 20. The profile is currently missing the majority of information Google needs to rank the business confidently.

2. Restart the review engine. The most recent review is over a year old. Build a simple system — a follow-up text or email with a review link sent after every completed job — and aim for 2–3 new reviews per week. Review velocity is one of the top 3 local ranking factors and right now it is at zero.

3. Build content on the website. Go from 6 indexed pages to at least 30. Create dedicated pages for every service Anthony's Hughes Tree & Stump Grinding offers and area pages for the neighborhoods and towns they serve. Each new page is a new opportunity to show up when someone searches for a specific service in a specific location.

Bonus: Add the business address to the website footer using the exact GBP formatting (including "WY"), embed a Google Map, fix the H1 tag to include keyword plus city, add diverse and geotagged photos, build citations on Apple Maps, Bing Places, Nextdoor, and BBB, and improve website speed through backend optimization.
