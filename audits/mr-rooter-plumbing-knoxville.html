How Mr. Rooter Plumbing of Knoxville Ranks Right Now

We started by Googling "Mr. Rooter Plumbing of Knoxville" to see what shows up. They do rank first for their own business name, and no other companies with a similar name appear to be competing for that search. However, we did notice a Crossville, Tennessee location appearing in results, which suggests there may be some geographic confusion in Google's understanding of this business. That ties into the NAP consistency issue we will cover below.

For the main keyword search — "plumbing in Knoxville Tennessee" — Mr. Rooter Plumbing of Knoxville does not currently appear in the top results of the Google Maps pack. Plumbing in Knoxville is a highly competitive market, and breaking into the top 3 requires a combination of strong GBP optimization, consistent NAP data, and targeted website content. There is room for improvement here, and the fixes are within reach.

Mr. Rooter Plumbing of Knoxville Google Business Profile Review

Mr. Rooter Plumbing of Knoxville has a decent review presence, though the velocity could be stronger. We recommend aiming for at least 2–3 new Google reviews per week for service-based businesses. To put the importance of velocity in context, during another audit we conducted for a plumbing business in Powell, the number one ranked business had only around 56 reviews while the number three ranked business had over 800. Total count alone does not determine rankings — consistency and recency carry significant weight.

Their GBP photos are real and include some professional shots, which is good. None are stock images. The diversity of photo types could be expanded — more job-site photos, before-and-after shots, and team photos would strengthen the visual presence. Geotagging photos with GPS coordinates from specific job locations is a small effort that can help reinforce the business's service area in Google's eyes.

Mr. Rooter Plumbing of Knoxville has five categories set on their GBP. This is more than we recommend. For the vast majority of businesses, one primary category — in this case "Plumber" — is ideal, with at most two or three secondary categories. When categories are too similar or too numerous, they can dilute the ranking power of the primary keyword rather than strengthening it. We would recommend pruning this down to just "Plumber" as the primary category and removing the rest. The services those categories describe belong in the GBP services section instead.

Speaking of services — Mr. Rooter Plumbing of Knoxville does not appear to have any services listed on their profile. Google allows up to 99, and we recommend adding at least 30 with descriptions for the top 20. This is where you capture long-tail searches. Someone searching "sewer line repair Knoxville" or "tankless water heater installation near me" will not find Mr. Rooter Plumbing of Knoxville through categories — they will find them through services.

The Q&A / FAQ section is also empty. Adding 5–10 common customer questions with clear answers builds trust and provides Google with more keyword-rich content directly on the profile.

We noticed Mr. Rooter Plumbing of Knoxville has two separate booking links set up, possibly from different ad campaigns or A/B testing. We would recommend consolidating to a single booking link to avoid confusion and keep tracking clean.

Mr. Rooter Plumbing of Knoxville Website SEO Review

The Mr. Rooter Plumbing of Knoxville website has a solid title tag: "Knoxville Plumbers, Knoxville Plumber Services." It includes the primary keyword and location, which is exactly what Google wants to see. We would not change the title tag itself, though adding the business name and extending it with more service keywords could strengthen it further.

The H1 tag reads "Your Local Knoxville Plumbers," which includes the keyword and city. One improvement would be to include the business name in the H1 for stronger brand association in search results.

The most impactful finding on the website is a NAP inconsistency. The Google Business Profile lists the address with "DR" for Drive, while the website footer spells out the full word "Drive." This may seem trivial, but Google treats consistency as a trust signal. When the name, address, or phone number does not match exactly across every platform — website, GBP, Facebook, Yelp, Apple Maps — Google cannot be certain which version is correct. That uncertainty directly impacts local rankings. The fix is simple: change every instance of the address across the entire online presence to match the GBP formatting exactly.

The site has plenty of indexed pages — well above the 30-page minimum we recommend — with dedicated service pages like water heater repair and replacement that can capture specific search queries. The site loads quickly at around 1.2 seconds with all green indicators in PageSpeed Insights. It has a valid SSL certificate. And it does have LocalBusiness schema markup implemented, which puts Mr. Rooter Plumbing of Knoxville ahead of most competitors on the technical side.

Mr. Rooter Plumbing of Knoxville Citations & Local Listings

Mr. Rooter Plumbing of Knoxville has citations on several high-authority directories including HomeAdvisor, the Mr. Rooter corporate site, and other major platforms. The foundation is there, but there is room to build out the citation profile further. We would recommend adding listings on Apple Business Connect, Bing Places, Nextdoor, Foursquare, Yellow Pages, and plumbing-specific directories. Every consistent, high-quality citation builds the trust signal that strengthens Google's confidence in the listing.

What Mr. Rooter Plumbing of Knoxville Is Doing Well

There is genuine strength in this profile. Mr. Rooter Plumbing of Knoxville ranks first for their own business name with no competing brands. Their website is fast, has SSL, has schema markup, and has plenty of indexed content with dedicated service pages. They have a booking link in their GBP and real photos rather than stock images. Their title tag is well-structured. The site looks professionally built and the technical fundamentals are sound.

Top Recommendations for Mr. Rooter Plumbing of Knoxville

1. Fix the NAP inconsistency immediately. Change "Drive" to "DR" (or vice versa) so the address matches the GBP exactly — on the website footer, Facebook, Yelp, and every other directory listing. This is the single highest-impact fix because it is currently undermining trust signals across the entire online presence.

2. Prune categories and load services. Remove excess GBP categories and keep only "Plumber" as the primary. Move everything else into the services section — add at least 30 services with descriptions for the top 20. This captures the long-tail searches that categories cannot.

3. Add the Q&A section and consolidate booking links. Populate the GBP Q&A with 5–10 common customer questions. Reduce the two booking links down to one clean landing page.

Bonus: Add the business name to the H1 tag, geotag photos with Knoxville-area coordinates, build out additional citations on Nextdoor, Apple Maps, and Bing Places, and increase review velocity to a consistent 2–3 per week.
